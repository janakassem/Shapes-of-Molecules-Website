Shapes of Molecules

To figure out the shape of a molecule we must first figure out the regions of electron density. These are lone pairs or bonded atoms.
Ammonia has four regions of electron density:
One lone pair
Three bonded atoms
	
Methane has four regions of electron density:
No lone pairs
Four bonded atoms
	
Water has four regions of electron density:
Two lone pairs
Two bonded atoms
The shape of the molecule depends on how many regions there are of each type.

When there are only bonded atoms:

Number of Bonded Atoms Shape of the Molecule

3 trigonal planar
4 tetrahedral

6 octahedral

When there are lone pairs and bonded atoms:

This is also influenced by the hybridization of the central atom.

How to find the hybridization of a central atom:

1. Find the central atom.
2. Draw an energy level diagram of the atom's valence shell.
3. Use the Aufbau Principle to add the correct number of electrons in the outermost shell.
4. Count the number of regions of electron density. That is how many orbitals you will need.
5. Combine s, p, d and f orbitals until you have a total number of orbitals used equal to the number that you need.
6. Add the electrons into the newly formed shells.
7. The hybridization is the number of each type of orbital used.
